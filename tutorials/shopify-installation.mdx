<Note>
  The Filecheck app is live on the [Shopify App Store](https://apps.shopify.com/filecheck). Installing it connects your store automatically — there are no API keys to copy and no code to write. Building something custom instead? You can also use the [Element and REST API](/tutorials/api-integration).
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the app">
    Install Filecheck from the [Shopify App Store](https://apps.shopify.com/filecheck). Installation connects your store and provisions your account for you — there are no keys to copy or paste.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start from onboarding">
    Once installed, you are taken straight to the Filecheck admin dashboard and an onboarding screen. From here you can either assign the built-in **default workflow** to a product to test quickly, or create your own **Workflow** and assign it. Assigning a workflow to a product is called **mapping**.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/shopify-onboarding.png" alt="Filecheck admin onboarding screen shown right after installing the Shopify app" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map a workflow to a product">
    Open **[Workflow Mappings](https://admin.filecheck.io/workflows/mappings)**. Your Shopify products are listed automatically (we sync them from your store). Click **Assign** next to a product and choose the **Workflow** to apply to it. Repeat for each product you want to gate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="See it on the product page">
    Visit a mapped product on your storefront. The Filecheck upload form appears automatically and **Add to cart** stays disabled until an uploaded file passes. Filecheck injects the form for you, so there are no theme edits to make.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download your files">
    After checkout, the validated, print-ready output is attached to the order in your Shopify admin, ready for fulfillment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **Not seeing the upload form on a mapped product?** By default Filecheck loads a script that finds the right spot on your product page and injects the upload form automatically, so in almost all cases you do **not** need to touch your theme. In rare cases (~0.5%) where a theme does not follow standard conventions — for example, no standard cart form — you may need to add the **Filecheck app block** to your product template in the theme editor. If a mapped product still does not show the form, [contact us](mailto:support@filecheck.io) and we will check it for you or add the block to that product page.
</Note>

For the technical data flow behind the app, see the [Shopify integration reference](/integrations/shopify).
